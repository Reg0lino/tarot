Conclusion: A Modern Construct

The analysis is clear: the 7-card 'Safe Passage' spread is a **modern invention**, created for the show. While it uses the ancient and positive symbolism of the pentagram, no documented tradition uses this specific 7-card layout. Historical pentagram spreads use 6 cards with entirely different meanings focused on the elements and spirit.

Italian-American Tarot Practices

The research shows that rather than creating unique spreads, Italian-Americans historically adopted widely used European methods. Their unique contribution was not in the *tools*, but in *how* these tools were woven into their existing framework of folk beliefs. The 'Why': Roots of Mysticism in the Immigrant Experience

Why did tarot and other mystical practices find such fertile ground in Italian-American communities, even among Catholics? The answer lies in the harsh realities of the immigrant experience and a resilient, pragmatic spirituality known as "folk Catholicism."

A Wave of Migration

The late 19th century saw a massive increase in Italian immigration, driven by economic hardship. This chart visualizes the scale of the influx to the U.S.

The Path to Folk Practice

Hardship & Alienation

Immigrants faced poverty, discrimination, and an unwelcoming formal church, creating intense anxiety and a need for control.

↓

Pragmatic "Folk Catholicism"

They relied on tangible, inherited folk practices like rituals to ward off the *malocchio* (evil eye), which offered immediate, practical solutions.

↓

Receptivity to Mysticism

This existing framework of practical spirituality made it easy to adopt other tools like Tarot, which promised guidance, order, and agency in a chaotic world.
